Here is the whole idea, with nothing hidden. The engine on the right never saw your screen — it never touched a pixel. It built this frame from a closed-form rule and judged it against named criteria (golden-angle spacing, balance, coverage), writing down how well each one was met. You, on the left, see only the image. Two ways of looking at the same thing.

Between us sits a certificate. It isn't my opinion and it isn't yours: it's a number — how far the frame sits from the bar — and a fixed rule for turning that number into a verdict. You don't have to take my word that it's right. Press re-check and your own browser re-derives the verdict from the certificate's own evidence. Move the slider and watch it flip exactly where the rule says — at 0.60, not where I'd prefer.

That's the point of the thing. Not trust the model, not trust me — a frame we can both stand in front of, and a verdict either of us can check.
