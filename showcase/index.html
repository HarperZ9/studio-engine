Talk to the model about this frame

It answers only what it can prove from this frame — every claim is a number you can re-derive. A live, open-ended conversation runs on the local engine; here the witness speaks from what it measured.

Here is the whole idea, with nothing hidden. The engine on the right never saw your screen — it never touched a pixel. It built this frame from a closed-form rule and judged it against named criteria (golden-angle spacing, balance, coverage), writing down how well each one was met. You, on the left, see only the image. Two ways of looking at the same thing.

Between us sits a certificate. It isn't my opinion and it isn't yours: it's a number — how far the frame sits from the bar — and a fixed rule for turning that number into a verdict. The engine that judges runs right here in your browser, so you don't have to take my word: press re-derive and it reproduces the verdict from the certificate's own evidence.

And the frame isn't fixed — it's a shared substrate we both actuate. You move a real parameter; the model makes its own improving move; after every change the certificate re-derives, live and honest. Push it off a clean value and the verdict turns refuted — at the engine's bar, not where anyone would prefer. Then talk to the model about what just happened, and check every word it says.

That's the point of the thing. Not trust the model, not trust me — a frame we can both shape and both stand in front of, and a verdict either of us can check.
